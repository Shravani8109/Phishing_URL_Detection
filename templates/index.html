What is a Phishing URL?

A phishing URL is a fake web address designed to look like it belongs to a trusted site.Scammers use it to trick people into giving away personal information,like passwords or credit card numbers, by making them think they're on a legitimate website.

Why you should avoid Phishing URL?

Identity Theft:

Phishing URLs can steal your personal information like passwords or credit card details,leading to identity theft and financial loss.

Malware Attacks:

Clicking on phishing links can infect your device with harmful software,allowing hackers to access your files, track your activities,or even take control of your device.

Financial Fraud:

Phishing URLs often trick you into giving away banking information,enabling fraudsters to drain your accounts or make unauthorized purchases.

Privacy Breach:

Providing personal information on phishing websites can compromise your privacy,exposing sensitive data to cybercriminals who may misuse it.

Reputation Damage:

Falling victim to phishing can damage your reputation,as scammers may use your compromised accounts to send spam or conduct fraudulent activities in your name.
